My Recent Work on HLSL Shader(Unity)

　Cross-hatching simulation had long be discussed in the history of Computer Graphics. Many researchers had suggested the better-look shading of hatching for a long period of time. In 2001, the new paper came out with its name called ‘Real-Time Hatching’ authored by Emil Praun, Hugues Hoppe, Matthew Webb, and Adam Finkelstein. They introduced how to render cross-hatching in real time. The keypoint of their algorithm is using TAM which is short for Tonal Art Map. Prepare 6 textures of different kinds of pencile-strokes, and bind them into two textures. Since we can store datas to RGB each, it actually shortens computations.
　The picture shown on the left tells the result of their algorithm. I tested their method to see how accurate the hatching will be. The result is within satisfactory, however, there needs to be more fixes to make it more realistic. Thus, that is my next work for this project.
